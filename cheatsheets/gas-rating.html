Gas Rating

Source: S3-10 to S3-15
ℹ️

Before gas rating: operate the appliance at its appropriate gas rate and ensure it is the only appliance operating on the installation.

Method 1 — Imperial (U6) meter using test dial S3-10/11

Time one complete revolution of the test dial (in seconds, then convert to decimal minutes).

Gas Rate
ft³/hr = 60 × Size of Test Dial (ft³) ÷ Time (minutes, decimal)
Heat Input (Btu/hr)
Btu/hr = 61,290 × Size of Test Dial ÷ Time (minutes, decimal)
Heat Input (kW gross)
kW = 1,077 × Size of Test Dial ÷ Time (seconds)
Alternative formula (multiple revolutions)
ft³/hr = 3,600 × Dial size × Number of revs ÷ Time taken (seconds) → × C.V. → Btu/hr → ÷ 3,412 → kW
Worked Example — 1 ft³ dial, 1 revolution in 45 seconds (= 0.75 min)
Gas Rate = 60 × 1 ÷ 0.75 = 80 ft³/hr Btu/hr = 61,290 × 1 ÷ 0.75 = 81,720 Btu/hr kW (gross) = 1,077 × 1 ÷ 45 = 24 kW

Method 2 — E6 Ultrasonic / direct-reading metric meter S3-13/14

⚠️

The E6 display does not increment evenly. Always measure over a 2 minute period for reasonable accuracy.

1Record the LCD reading (last 3 digits are for gas rating — 10ths, 100ths, 1000ths of m³).
2After exactly 2 minutes, record the LCD reading again.
3Deduct first reading from second to get gas flow in m³ over 2 minutes.
4Apply formula or use conversion table.
kW (gross) from m³ in 2 minutes
kW = 3,600 × volume (m³) × 10.8 ÷ 120 = volume (m³) × 324 (C.V. of natural gas ≈ 10.8 kW per m³, i.e. 38.8 MJ/m³ ÷ 3.6)
Worked Example — Reading increases 0.991 → 0.936 … wait, correct reading from guide:
End reading: 0.991 m³ (last 3 digits) Start reading: 0.936 m³ Difference: 0.055 m³ in 2 minutes kW = 3,600 × 0.055 × 10.8 ÷ 120
= 17.82 kW (gross)

Net → Gross
Gross = Net × 1.11

Net = Gross ÷ 1.11
