Ventilation Sizing

Source: S5-6 to S5-48

Step 0 — Calculate room volume S5-7

Room volume is needed before you can check flueless appliance limits or space heater maximums.

Room Volume
Volume (m³) = Length (m) × Width (m) × Height (m)
ℹ️

A room is generally not less than 5 m³. If people are likely to spend time in a space, treat it as a room. If in doubt, treat it as a compartment — compartment rules are more stringent.

Example — room 4m × 3m × 2.4m
Volume = 4 × 3 × 2.4
= 28.8 m³

Route A — Open flued appliance in a room S5-7

A fixed adventitious allowance of 35 cm² (equivalent to 7 kW) is assumed, because the flue draws air in through gaps around doors and windows. Only the input above 7 kW needs a purpose-provided vent.

Required vent free area (cm²)
Vent = (Net heat input (kW) − 7) × 5
✅

Net input ≤ 7 kW: no purpose-provided vent required.

🚫

Adventitious allowance does NOT apply to flueless appliances or compartments.

Example A1 — 23 kW open-flued boiler in a kitchen
Vent = (23 − 7) × 5 = 16 × 5
= 80 cm²
Example A2 — 6 kW open-flued wall heater
Input ≤ 7 kW → adventitious air is sufficient
No vent required
⚠️

If the input is given as gross (common on older appliances): convert first.
Net = Gross ÷ 1.11. Then apply (Net − 7) × 5.

Route B — Compartment ventilation: Table 4 S5-36

Compartments need both a high-level and a low-level vent. No adventitious allowance — use the full kW in all calculations.

Air vents must communicate with the same room or space, or both be on the same outside wall. High and low vents should be as far apart vertically as possible.

Example B1 — 24 kW open-flued boiler in compartment, air from room
High level: 24 × 10 = 240 cm² Low level: 24 × 20 = 480 cm²
Example B2 — same boiler, air direct from outside
High level: 24 × 5 = 120 cm² Low level: 24 × 10 = 240 cm²

🚫

For open-flued appliances in compartments: vents must not communicate with a bathroom, shower room, or (for new installs after Jan 1996) a bedroom — unless the appliance is < 14 kW and fitted with a vitiation sensing device or thermal spillage detector.

⚠️

For range-rated appliances: size vents on maximum heat input, not the set input.

Route C — Flueless appliances: Table 1 S5-7/8

Flueless appliances burn gas in the room itself. Three things must be established: appliance type, room volume, and whether there is a door or window direct to outside. No adventitious allowance applies.

ℹ️

All flueless appliance installations also require an openable window, hinged panel, adjustable louvre, or equivalent that opens directly to outside air — in addition to any permanent vent. If no openable window is available, mechanical or passive stack ventilation must be provided.

Calculating max permitted input for a flueless space heater S5-8

Before sizing the vent, you first need to confirm the appliance is not too large for the room.

In a room — max input limit
Max input (W) = 45 × Room volume (m³) Max input (kW) = 45 × Volume ÷ 1000
In an internal space — max input limit
Max input (W) = 90 × Room volume (m³) Max input (kW) = 90 × Volume ÷ 1000
Example C1 — Is a 1.2 kW space heater acceptable in a 23 m³ room?
Max input = 45 W/m³ × 23 m³ = 1,035 W = 1.035 kW Appliance is 1.2 kW
1.2 kW > 1.035 kW → appliance exceeds room limit. Not acceptable as-is.
Example C2 — Space heater vent size: 3.5 kW heater in a room
Vent = 100 + 55 × (3.5 − 2.7) = 100 + 55 × 0.8 = 100 + 44
= 144 cm² (plus openable window to outside)
Example C3 — Flueless instantaneous water heater, 11 kW, in a room of 8 m³
Room volume = 8 m³ → falls in "5 to 10" band
= 100 cm² vent required, direct to outside + openable window

Multi-appliance installations — choosing the correct total S5-9

When two or more appliances are in the same room or space, the required air vent is the largest of the following three values:

#Calculate…Method
1 Total flueless space heater requirement Sum the vent areas from Table 1 for all flueless space heaters
2 Total open flued space heater requirement Sum (kW − 7) × 5 for all open-flued space heaters
Exception: two similar open-flued gas fires both < 7 kW and total ≤ 14 kW in a through room with two chimneys → no vent normally needed. Does NOT apply to DFE fires.
3 Greatest individual rated input of any other single appliance Take the single highest vent requirement from any remaining appliance type
ℹ️

In multi-appliance installations, a space heater includes: central heating boiler, warm air unit, gas fire, or convector. A fire/back boiler combination uses the total combined maximum rated input.

⚠️

The permanent ventilation in a multi-appliance room should, wherever practicable, be sited between the appliances.

Example D1 — Kitchen: 23 kW open-flued boiler + flueless oven in a 15 m³ room
Open-flued boiler: (23 − 7) × 5 = 80 cm² Flueless oven: room >10 m³ = Nil cm² (from Table 1) ───────────────────────────────────── Route 1 (flueless total): 0 cm² Route 2 (open flued total): 80 cm² Route 3 (greatest individual): 80 cm²
Required vent = 80 cm² (largest value)
Example D2 — Lounge: 23 kW open-flued boiler + 3.5 kW flueless space heater in a 25 m³ room
Max heater input for room = 45 × 25 ÷ 1000 = 1.125 kW → 3.5 kW exceeds limit! Assuming heater is within room limit: Flueless space heater vent: 100 + 55 × (3.5 − 2.7) = 144 cm² Open-flued boiler vent: (23 − 7) × 5 = 80 cm² ───────────────────────────────────── Route 1 (flueless total): 144 cm² Route 2 (open flued total): 80 cm² Route 3 (greatest individual): 144 cm²
Required vent = 144 cm² (largest value)

⚠ Always check the space heater isn't oversized for the room first (45 W/m³ rule).

Measuring existing air vents S5-39/40

Grilles must allow a 5 mm ball through but stop a 10 mm ball. Always check vents are not blocked by cling film, cobwebs, insulating materials, or fly screens.

For a louvred vent: measure the perpendicular dimension, not the diagonal. (13 mm measured diagonally = only 10 mm perpendicular — use 10 mm.)

For two vents in series: use the free area of the smaller vent. Space between them must be clear.

For a drilled door vent: free area = drilled hole area only. If grille sits flush to door surface, area is reduced further and must be carefully assessed. Drilled door vents are inadvisable if heat input > 30 kW.

Free area of a rectangular hole grid
Area (mm²) = Hole width (mm) × Hole height (mm) × Number of holes Area (cm²) = Area (mm²) ÷ 100
Example — grille with 9 rectangular holes each 10 mm × 20 mm
Area = 10 × 20 × 9 = 1,800 mm² = 1,800 ÷ 100
= 18 cm²
