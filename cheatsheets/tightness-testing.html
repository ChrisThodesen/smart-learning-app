Tightness Testing

Source: S2-5 to S2-18

Test procedure at a glance S2-9/10/11

1Open appliance isolation valves. Close all appliance control taps (and lift cooker fold-down lid).
2Turn off gas at the appropriate supply control valve (ECV / AECV for MOP ≤ 75 mbar · MIV for MOP > 75 mbar).
3Connect pressure gauge to suitable test point or test T-piece.
4Carry out 1 minute let-by test: adjust to 7–10 mbar, close valve, watch for any pressure rise.
5Raise pressure to 20–21 mbar. (Do not exceed 21 mbar to avoid regulator lock-up. If 21–23 mbar, readjust down. If > 23 mbar, drop to 7–10 mbar first.)
6Allow 1 minute stabilisation. Readjust to 20–21 mbar if needed.
7Check for perceptible pressure fall over 2 minutes.
8Pass / fail decision (see tables below). Record results.
9Remove gauge, reseal test point. Test all exposed joints with LDF. Purge if required.

Definition of "perceptible movement" S2-7

Gauge typePass threshold
Water (U-gauge)Any visible movement = perceptible (≈ >0.25 mbar)
Electronic (1 decimal)0.2 mbar or less = not perceptible
Electronic (0.1 mbar)0.25 mbar or less = not perceptible
ℹ️

For new installations and all let-by tests, the pass criterion is no perceptible movement at all.

Let-by test S2-9

Set to 7–10 mbar · close valve · watch 1 minute.

ResultAction
No riseValve passed — proceed with test
Pressure risesValve letting-by. Check with LDF on barrel/ball. ECV let-by → notify National Gas Emergency. MIV let-by → notify MAM immediately.

⚠️

Permissible drops only apply to existing installations where at least one appliance is connected and there is no smell of gas. New installations, or existing with no appliances, require no perceptible movement.

ℹ️

Where there is no meter (e.g. unmetered flat), use the E6/no meter column. The larger allowance reflects the small volume of pipework in that scenario.

Lock-up S2-8

Lock-up occurs when the regulator valve is forced onto its seating (no flow, full shut-off). During a tightness test this means the section upstream of the regulator (meter inlet) is not tested.

⚠️

To avoid lock-up, never exceed 21 mbar when raising test pressure. If exceeded: drop to 7–10 mbar before re-raising. If exceeded 23 mbar: must drop to 7–10 mbar before re-raising.
