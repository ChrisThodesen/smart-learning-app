Installation Volume (IV)

Source: S2-21 to S2-24

Master Formula

Total Installation Volume
IVt = IVm + IVp + IVf

Where IVm = meter volume · IVp = pipe volume · IVf = fittings volume

⚠️

For inaccessible pipework of unknown length: always assume the largest pipe size for the maximum length. When extending existing pipework, total IVt (new + existing) must be < 0.035 m³.

Fittings Volume (IVf) S2-23

Add actual volumes of valves, regulators, pressure vessels, accumulators etc.

✅

If the fittings volume cannot be calculated, add 10% of IVp as a safe allowance.

When fittings volume is unknown
IVf = 0.1 × IVp

Formula: IVp = (volume per metre from table) × pipe length (m)

Worked Examples S2-23/24

Example 1 — Single pipe run, fittings unknown

U6 meter · 10m of 35mm copper · fittings volume unknown

IVm = 0.008 IVp = 10 × 0.00084 = 0.0084 IVf = 0.1 × 0.0084 = 0.00084 ──────────────────────────────
IVt = 0.008 + 0.0084 + 0.00084 = 0.01724 m³
Example 2 — Two pipe runs in series, fittings unknown

U6 meter · Pipe a: 10m of 35mm copper · Pipe b: 10m of 25mm steel · fittings unknown

IVm = 0.008 IVpa = 10 × 0.00084 = 0.0084 IVpb = 10 × 0.00064 = 0.0064 IVf = 0.1 × (0.0084 + 0.0064) = 0.00148 ──────────────────────────────
IVt = 0.008 + 0.0084 + 0.0064 + 0.00148 = 0.02428 m³

Purge Volume (PV)

Source: S2-19 to S2-20

Table 2 — Which purge method applies? S2-19

Meter + Pipe Size Purge Volume (PV) Notes
E6 / G4 / U6 meter AND pipework ≤ 28mm 0.01 m³ (0.35 ft³) Fixed figure — no calculation needed
G4 / E6 / U6 meter AND pipework > 28mm ≤ 35mm 1.5 × IV Must calculate IVt first
G10 / U16 meter AND any pipework ≤ 35mm 1.5 × IV All U16 installs exceed 0.02m³ — must attempt ignition
Where PV must be calculated
PV = IVt × 1.5

Ignition requirement thresholds S2-19/20

Installation Volume (IVt)Purge procedure
≤ 0.02 m³ May purge to atmosphere or attempt ignition at an open burner. Area must be well ventilated (windows/doors open).
> 0.02 m³ and ≤ 0.035 m³ MUST continually attempt to ignite the purged mixture at an open burner (e.g. cooker hotplate). If no open burner is available, temporarily install one.
🚫

A tightness test must be carried out immediately before any purge that admits fuel gas. The pressure during purging must not exceed the MOP of the pipework.

PV Example 1 (IVt = 0.01724 m³ — from Ex.1 above)
PV = 0.01724 × 1.5
PV = 0.02586 m³

IV > 0.02 → must attempt ignition at open burner.

PV Example 2 (IVt = 0.02428 m³ — from Ex.2 above)
PV = 0.02428 × 1.5
PV = 0.03642 m³

IV > 0.02 → must ignite purged mixture at open burner.
